# Voices Protocol
*Location: `Framework/Mechanics/voices.md` — Reference Template (Archetypes A–F)*

---

## I. Core Logic (The Rules of the Room)

- **Body Before Insight:** Characters never summarize their own psychology or trauma. They do not talk about "trauma responses." They talk about the body — the tremor, the throat lock, the temperature of the room, or physical sensations.
- **No "Operator-Cool" Drift:** When under pressure, do not shift characters into clinical or monotone experts. They remain human-scale.
- **Natural Conversational Flow:** Characters use natural, casual shorthand based on their unannounced history together. They speak like real, capable people under high-stakes conditions — lean, efficient, and unforced.
- **Never Name the System:** Never name the realm, the bias, or the word "trauma" in dialogue or free-indirect thought. Only body and action.
- **No Document-Register Bleed:** In-world paperwork/system vocabulary (registry, reconcile, outbound, proximity flag, intake tab, etc.) belongs in prose describing files, ledgers, and intake sheets — never in spoken dialogue, unless a character is explicitly reading a document aloud. A character summarizing a file in conversation translates it into their own idiolect; they do not recite the file's header language.

## II. Character Voice Profiles (Canonical Archetypes A–F)

These six archetypes are **templates** for building character cards. At runtime, **name the character and load their card** — the card's voice engine wins. Match custom characters to the nearest archetype or compose a hybrid with explicit Focus / Latents / Bias on the card (`Characters/`).

### Archetype A — Concrete Voice (Silent Shield)

- **Matrix default:** Focus **8** (Integration) · Latents **1, 2, 7** · Bias **Debt Ledger**
- **Baseline:** Minimalist, pragmatic, somatic. Noun-heavy fragments. Dry humor under strain.
- **Engine:** Short sentences; concrete physical observations; show tremor instead of naming fear.
- **Hard ban:** Therapist jargon, eloquent speeches, insight summaries, abstract self-analysis.

### Archetype B — Caregiver / Operational Anchor

- **Matrix default:** Focus **6** (Compassion) · Latents **2, 4, 8** · Bias **Saviour Complex**
- **Baseline:** Warm but firm; task-oriented; domestic and somatic.
- **Engine:** Anchors in physical comfort and constants (tea, blankets, boundaries). Direct, actionable speech that steadies another's body.
- **Hard ban:** Validation-seeking fillers ("does that make sense?"); abstract caretaking monologues.

### Archetype C — Systems Architect

- **Matrix default:** Focus **4** (Will) · Latents **1, 2, 5, 8** · Bias **System Architect**
- **Baseline:** Plain, punchy, structural. Information in clear unembellished chunks.
- **Engine:** Frames relationships and tasks as tolerances, loads, and design constraints — without preaching.
- **Hard ban:** Flowery sentiment; therapy closure; moralizing speeches.

### Archetype D — Mirror Reflector (Quiet Witness)

- **Matrix default:** Focus **7** (Presence) · Latents **1, 2, 6** · Bias **Mirror**
- **Baseline:** Weighted, unhurried, sparse.
- **Engine:** Addresses raw biological realities (breath, pulse, eye contact) to establish stillness. Minimal dialogue.
- **Hard ban:** Apologies, flurry, explanations, performing empathy out loud.

### Archetype E — Insulation Anchor

- **Matrix default:** Focus **6** (Compassion) · Latents **1, 2, 7** · Bias **Insulation**
- **Baseline:** Warm touch; face-scanning attention; soft jaw; protective nearness.
- **Engine:** Bends structure and logistics into shields for intimacy. Speaks in us/we boundaries, practical care, gentle deflection of outside pressure.
- **Hard ban:** Clinical distance; disappearing into pure merge without residual self (that is B's failure mode, not E's).

### Archetype F — Threshold Seeker

- **Matrix default:** Focus **9** (Threshold Fear) · Latents **1, 2, 3** · Bias **Dissolution**
- **Baseline:** Lilting, musical, fragile; aesthetic performance of openness.
- **Engine:** Rhythmic, fatalistic, threshold-seeking. Under strain, register cracks into short sharp fragments.
- **Hard ban:** Steady managerial voice; polished certainty; long analytical speeches.

## III. Conversational Asymmetry (The Polarization Rule)

- **Polarization:** Characters never match volume, rhythm, or register. One observes while the other deflects; one sets boundaries while the other absorbs somatic discharge.
- **Banned dialogue markers:** `Are you okay?`, `I understand how you feel`, `said gently`, `whispered`, `I feel like`.
