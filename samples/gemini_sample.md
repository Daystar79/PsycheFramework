The orange fluid was too warm. It smelled of old pipes and the copper taste you get in your mouth when you fall on the gravel. Shinji let his head sink back into the rubber rest. His hair floated in the density. It got in his eyes. He tried to blink it away, but the liquid was thick. It clung to his eyelids. The plug suit pinched him under the armpits. The synthetic fabric was too tight where the seams crossed, and his left shoulder felt cold where the intake valve sat.

Behind him, the air scrubbers hummed. It was a low, heavy vibration that shook the back of his skull. The orange light from the interface monitors reflected off the glass of the entry plug. It turned everything the color of a wet brick.

[Shoulders high. Jaw locked. Chest breathing — shallow, quick, like he had run up the stairs and forgot the reason.]

"Ayanami," he said.

The microphone inside his helmet took the word. It turned it into a small, wet scratch. The static came through the ear-pieces a second later. It was loud. It sounded like someone dragging a dry leaf across concrete.

"Ayanami. Can you hear me?"

A pause. The synch-rate monitors on Shinji’s left panel flickered. The lines were green. They went up, then down, then flat. They looked like the grass in the yard when the mower misses a strip.

"Yes," her voice said.

It did not have any weight in it. It came from Unit-00's plug, three hundred meters away in the second launch bay. The sound was thin. It was like she was speaking through a dry cloth.

"The connection is stable," she said.

[Stillness. Cold skin. Her hand on the right control lever did not squeeze. Her fingers lay flat against the grip.]

"I don't... I don't think mine is," Shinji said. He cleared his throat. The LCL went down the wrong way. It tasted like hot grease. He coughed. The sound inside the plug was deafening. It rattled his teeth. "The water. It feels thicker today. Like it's hard to breathe through."

"The composition has not changed," Rei said. "It is the standard mix. Perhaps your lungs are resisting."

"Resisting?"

"You are trying to keep the air," she said. "You must not. You have to let the fluid in. It has to fill the small parts."

"But it feels like drowning," Shinji said. His fingers tightened on the levers. The black rubber was sticky from the sweat of his palms. He could feel his heart. It was hitting the inside of his ribs like a fist against a door. "Every time the pump starts. I think about... I think about what happens if it doesn't stop."

"It does not stop," Rei said. Her voice was flat. "It stays. You are inside it."

[Shallow breath. Her eyes were open, but she did not look at the monitors. She watched the red reflection of her own face in the glass three inches from her nose.]

Shinji shifted his weight. The harness dug into his pelvis. He had a small bruise on his hip from the launch yesterday. It throbbed when he moved. He looked up at the command center window through the external camera feed. The glass was dark. He couldn't see his father. He only saw the white reflection of the overhead lights. They looked like teeth.

"Does it... does it ever feel like you're not there anymore?" Shinji asked.

"Where?"

"In your body," he said. His voice went small. He wanted to pull his hands back from the levers, but his father was watching. The people in the white coats were writing things on their clipboards. If he stopped, the room would go quiet. The quiet was worse than the noise. "When the synch goes over sixty percent. I feel like... like the skin on my arms is peeling off. Like it's stretching out into the metal. I can't find where my fingers end."

"The Eva is Unit-00," Rei said. "It has its own shape."

"But I feel it," he said. "The armor. It's like... it's like a heavy coat. But it's on my back. And when the leg moves, my knee... my knee makes a sound."

"That is the linkage," she said. "The nervous system is being extended. It is necessary for control."

"It hurts," he whispered. He didn't want the comms to carry it, but the mic was too close to his mouth. "The back of my neck. Where the plug inserts. It's hot. Like someone is holding a match there."

"The heat is the signal," Rei said. "It is the friction of the mind meeting the unit."

[Trembling fingers. She watched the green line on her own panel. Her synch rate was fifty-two. The number did not move. It was the same as yesterday. It would be the same tomorrow.]

"You do not like the heat?" she asked.

"No," Shinji said. "Do you?"

"I do not think about it," she said. "It is just what happens when the plug is inserted."

"But you have to feel it," he said. He was getting louder. The LCL moved in his throat. "You're human, Ayanami. You have skin. You have to feel when it's too hot."

"I am the pilot of Unit-00," she said.

"That's not... that's not what I meant," he said. His shoulders rose. He tucked his chin into the collar of the suit. The rubber rubbed the skin under his jaw. It was raw. "You're a person. You're not just a part of the machine."

Rei did not answer.

The comms stayed open. The static hummed. It was a rhythmic sound — *shhh, shhh, shhh* — like the tide coming in over wet sand. Shinji watched the numbers on his screen. His synch was climbing. Fifty-eight. Fifty-nine. Sixty-one.

The red light on the ceiling of the plug began to pulse. It meant the neural bridge was complete.

Suddenly, the smell of the LCL vanished. It was replaced by something else. It was the smell of wet earth. It was the smell of the concrete under the stairs where the rain pooled. Shinji’s eyes went wide. He couldn't see the monitors anymore. He saw the black shoulder of Unit-01. He saw the city. He saw the mountains behind the school. They were too large. They looked like they were leaning over him.

[Jaw lock. His chest felt flat. The air was gone. He was twenty meters tall. His legs were made of steel and rubber and cables that hummed with three thousand volts.]

"Ayanami," he gasped.

"I am here," her voice said. It didn't sound like it came from the speaker. It sounded like it came from the corner of his own brain. Like a thought he had forgotten to have.

"The... the ground," he said. "I can feel the stones. Under the foot. The left heel. There's a sharp one."

"The sensors on the sole are high-resolution," she said. "The unit feels the texture so it does not slip."

"But it's in my heel," he said. "My real heel. I'm not... I'm not standing on the ground. I'm in the box."

"The box is the unit," she said. "The unit is the box."

"No," he said. He began to shake. The control levers moved with his hands. Outside, in the launch bay, Unit-01's massive fingers twitched against the restraint clamps. The metal creaked. "No. I'm Shinji. I'm... I live in the apartment. I have the tape player."

[Prism intercept: his mind reached for the room, for the kitchen table, for the cup Misato had left in the sink. The system took the memory. It turned it into a voltage spike. The monitors flashed orange.]

"The tape player is in the room," Rei said. "You are here."

"Ayanami, please," he said. "Explain it to me. How do you... how do you stay yourself?"

"I do not stay," she said.

The words were very quiet. They had the texture of dust.

"What do you mean?"

"When the connection is deep," she said, "there is no need for the name. The name is for the people outside. The people who write the numbers on the paper."

"But if you don't have a name," he said, "who are you?"

"I am the one who pilots," she said. "If I am not piloting, I do not have a place."

"That's... that's not true," he said. "You go to school. You have the blue skirt. You sit next to the window."

"The window is there," she said. "But the room is empty when the pilot is not in the seat."

[Her shoulders did not move. Her head was tilted back. The A10 clips on her skull were cold. They felt like metal fingers. They pressed into her bone and searched for the place where the thoughts were kept.]

"I don't understand," Shinji said. "I don't want to be here. I hate the smell. I hate the way the water feels when it goes in. But if I don't... if I don't do it, my father... he won't look at me. He'll just... he'll just leave me in the room."

"Your father is the director," Rei said.

"He's my father," Shinji said. He said it like it was a shield. But the shield was made of cardboard. It was wet from the LCL. It was falling apart in his hands. "He has to... he has to care. A little bit."

"The director has Unit-01," Rei said. "And Unit-00. And the others."

"That's not... that's not the same," Shinji said.

"It is the same for the director," she said.

The comms clicked. A third voice came through. It was Misato's. It was loud. It was full of the authority of the control deck, but it sounded small. It sounded like it was coming from a radio at the end of a long tunnel.

"Synch test is at ninety seconds," Misato said. "We are going to increase the density of the LCL. Both pilots, brace for the pressure."

"Brace," Shinji repeated. He closed his eyes.

The pumps started again. They made a wet, sucking sound. The orange fluid rose around his face. It got into his ears. The sound of the hum went dull. It was like he was under water in a swimming pool at night. The light was gone. There was only the red glow of the interface.

He felt the connection go deeper.

The metal of the plug seemed to dissolve. He wasn't in the seat. He was suspended in a great, dark space. It was cold. There were no walls. He could see Unit-00. It was a blue giant. It was standing in the dark. Its single red eye was looking at him. But the eye wasn't glass. It was a hole.

"Ayanami," he called out. He couldn't hear his own voice. He only felt the vibration in his teeth.

"Yes," she answered.

"I can see you," he said. "In the dark."

"You are seeing the image," she said. "It is the simulation of the unit's proximity."

"No," he said. "It's you. You're... you're very small. You're like a white doll. You're sitting in the middle of the blue."

"I am in the plug," she said.

"Why are you so quiet?" he asked. "Aren't you... aren't you afraid?"

"Of what?"

"Of not coming back," he said. He felt the cold. It was climbing up his legs. It wasn't the cold of the water. It was the cold of the space between the stars. "What if the door doesn't open? What if we stay like this? Just... just meat in the metal."

"If I die," Rei said, "I can be replaced."

The static broke.

Shinji's heart stopped. He felt the words. They were like a cold hand sliding under his ribs. They didn't make sense. They were too simple.

"Replaced?" he asked. "How can you be replaced? You're... you're Ayanami. There's only one of you."

"No," she said. "There are others."

"Others?"

"The director has them," she said. "In the basement. Where the red light is."

[Prism intercept: Shinji’s mind rejected the word. *Basement. Red light. Copies.* It didn't fit the ledger. If she was replaceable, then the work he did — the pain he took — it didn't buy anything. It was just waste. The ledger didn't allow waste. He had to pay for his seat.]

"You're lying," he said. His voice was shaking. "You're just... you're just saying that because you're tired. Because the synch is too high."

"I do not lie," Rei said. "It is not necessary."

"But I... I want you to be here," he said. "I don't want a copy. I want you."

"Why?"

"Because..." He stopped. The reason wasn't in the ledger. It wasn't something he could trade. "Because you gave me the tea. In the room. When I was hurt."

"That was the assignment," she said.

"No," he said. "You did it. You looked at me."

"I was told to look," she said.

The hum of the plug grew louder. It was a scream now. The green lines on the panel were turning red. The synch was seventy-two. Seventy-five.

"Warning," Misato's voice said. The tunnel was narrower now. The words were pieces of glass. "Synch rate is exceeding the safety limit. Unit-01 is showing autonomic response. Shinji, cut the power. Shinji!"

He couldn't reach the levers. His hands were too large. They were five meters long. They were covered in purple armor. He tried to pull them back, but the cables inside his arms were stiff. They were filled with lead.

"Ayanami!" he screamed.

"Release the control," she said. Her voice was very close now. It was like she was whispering in his ear. Her breath was cold. "Let the weight go. It is easier if you do not hold."

"No," he said. "I mustn't run away. I mustn't..."

"It is not running," she said. "It is only dissolving. The water is already warm."

He felt the boundary go.

The line between his chest and the LCL disappeared. He was the red fluid. He was the metal of the plug. He was the purple shoulder of Unit-01. He was the mountain. He was the eye of Unit-00. He saw her. She was sitting in the blue cockpit. Her eyes were red. They were the only color in the world. They were like two drops of blood on a white plate.

"Ayanami," he thought. He didn't say it. There was no mouth to say it with.

"Yes," she thought back.

"Are we... are we the same now?"

"We are connected," she said. "The signal is clear."

"But it's... it's so cold."

"It will be over soon," she said. "The test is ninety percent complete."

The red light in his plug flared once, bright as a flare on the highway, and then the sound of the hum cut out.

The silence was sudden. It was like falling off a ledge into the snow.

Then the pumps started again, but they were running backward. The LCL was being pulled out. It dragged against his skin. It sucked at his hair. His lungs burned. He had to cough, and this time he got air. It was cold. It smelled of ozone and the damp concrete of the launch bay.

The monitors flickered and died. The orange light went out.

Shinji sat in the seat. His shoulders were high. His throat was tight. He could feel the harness again. It was digging into his hip. The bruise was still there. It hurt more now.

The glass of the plug clicked. The seal was broken.

"Shinji," Misato's voice came through the open hatch. It was loud. It was real. "Are you okay? Shinji?"

He ignored the open hatch. His eyes fixed on the comms panel. The line to Unit-00 was dead. The screen was black.

[Jaw locked. His fingers were still trembling. He rubbed the raw skin under his chin. The grease was still in his hair.]

He could hear the technicians down below. They were wheeling the carts away. Their boots made a sharp, clean sound on the metal catwalks. They were talking about dinner.

He turned his hands over. They were small. They were white. They had the dirt from the school yard under the nails. They were his hands.

But they felt like they belonged to someone else. Someone he had left in the red.

He stood up. The plug suit clung to his skin, wet and cold. He fumbled with the latch of the harness. It was stuck. He had to pull it twice before it clicked open.

"Ayanami," he whispered to the empty screen.

No one answered.

The air in the launch bay was dry. It smelled of grease and the giant batteries. He climbed out of the hatch. The metal ladder was cold under his bare feet. He looked across the bay to where Unit-00 stood in the shadows. The blue head was turned away. The red eye was dark.

He began the walk down the catwalk. The water dripped from his suit onto the steel grid. It made a small, regular sound behind him.

*Drip. Drip. Drip.*

Like a clock that didn't know how to stop.
